Tulum Ruins Are One of Top 25 Landmarks in the World

Part of what makes the Riviera Maya such a great destination is the abundance of cultural activities available.
From cooking classes to historical sites, culture vultures have their appetites well and truly satisfied.The Mayan ruins are synonymous with the Riviera Maya and its rich culture. Attracting millions of visitors a year, the Tulum ruins are a true gem of the Riviera Maya and they are finally being recognised as such.

The top spot was taken by Angkor Wat in Siem Reap, Cambodia. This Indianised temple complex is the largest religious monument in the world. Other notable landmarks included Saint Peter’s Basilica in Vatican City, Italy as well as Machu Picchu in Peru and Golden Gate Bridge in San Francisco, USA. But for the first time, the Tulum ruins have crept into the 24th spot.

What attracts many visitors to the Tulum ruins above others is their uniqueness. First of all, they are thought to be one of the only well-preserved mesoamerican ruins located on a beach front. The Tulum ruins are perched on a clifftop overlooking the Caribbean sea which is 12 meters below. This makes for some stunning photo opportunities with rocky outcrops, sugary sand, azul sea and majestic ruins all in one! Moreover, before it’s current name, the city was called Zama or City of Dawn as this is the first place that the sunrise would reach in the Mayan world.
Eventually, the city was named Tulum to mean ‘wall’ as the Mayan site was an important port but also a strong fort in it’s lifetime during the years 1200 to 1450.
Nowadays, the Tulum ruins are the perfect day tour in the Riviera Maya. Daily tours start from sunrise, when all is quiet and you can really appreciate where the name Zama came from. Then the crowds descend and the ruins are momentarily repopulated until sunset.

TULUM IS CURRENTLY THE HOTTEST DESTINATION IN THE WORLD.
While the laid-back jungle charm is a huge factor in why people choose to visit this Caribbean paradise, it’s often the world-class bars and restaurants that are the real highlight of travellers’ time here in Tulum. Celebrities, socialites and people in the know choose to invest their time and money here. The variety and calibre of entertainment on offer is really what makes Tulum such a great place to invest in. Tulum real estate is set in a cutting-edge tropical paradise that presents boundless opportunities for work and play. There are a bunch of picture perfect places to spend a day with your toes in the sand, but not all beach bars are created equal. Here’s 3 truly magical places from low-key to infamous that will have you sipping and swimming all day and into the night.

Coco Beach - Tulum

Coco tulum is no local secret. The white-washed beach club perfectly compliments the cerulean sea and the combination is well documented on social media.

Papaya Playa Project

Papaya Playa Project markets themselves as ‘a truly sustainable project.’ But if there’s one thing Tulum understands, it’s that being environmentally friendly and having a ridiculously good time can go hand in hand.

Taqueria La Eufemia Tulum

In true Tulum fashion, La Eufemia is located on the Carreterra Tulum-Punta Allen and can only be found via a small painted sign above a gap in the trees. Follow this little, winding, sandy path over and under leaning palms and eventually you come to the beach - and Taqueria La Eufemia.
